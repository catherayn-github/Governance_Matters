Effect of Corporate Governance on Investment Efficiency: The Mediating Role of Audit Quality in Listed Holding Companies on the Philippine Stock Exchange for the Years 2017-2023

Introduction

Corporate governance plays a crucial role in shaping our investment environments, boosting investor confidence, and ensuring capital efficiency, particularly in today’s interconnected global market. Here in the Philippines, despite our country's economic growth and increasing foreign investments, we still face significant challenges, especially with local regulations. In this study, we explore the impact of the SEC's 2017 revised Code of Corporate Governance, focusing on how corporate governance, audit quality, and investment efficiency are interconnected from 2017 to 2023. By analyzing these factors, we aim to provide insights that can help enhance governance practices, improve audit standards, and promote greater investment efficiency in the Philippines. Furthermore, we have seen the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) take significant steps to strengthen corporate governance in the Philippines with the introduction of the updated Corporate Governance Code for publicly listed companies, effective January 1, 2017. Developed in collaboration with the International Finance Corporation (IFC), this revision aims to enhance the regulatory framework and improve the investment climate by promoting transparency, accountability, and integrity.

The 2017 Code is structured around sixteen principles, organized into five main sections, to reinforce corporate governance standards in the country

business
Board’s Governance Responsibilities (Principles 1-7)

We ensure that at least one-third of board members are independent to promote objectivity and protect minority shareholders. Our focus is on governance duties, proper oversight, and safeguarding shareholder interests.

account_balance
Disclosure and Transparency (Principles 8-11)

We prioritize transparency in financial and non-financial reporting, including submitting an annual corporate governance report to maintain accountability.

shield
Internal Control and Risk Management Framework (Principle 12)

We are committed to establishing effective internal controls and risk management systems to safeguard assets and improve operational efficiency.

people
Cultivating a Synergic Relationship with Shareholders (Principle 13)

We aim to build collaborative relationships with shareholders and engage with other stakeholders, such as employees and communities, ensuring their rights are respected.

group
Duties to Stakeholders (Principles 14-16)

We embrace our responsibilities toward stakeholders, promoting ethical practices in all dealings.

Research Abstract

In our study, we examined how audit quality mediates the relationship between corporate governance and investment efficiency among PSE-listed holding firms from 2017 to 2023. As businesses strive for sustainable growth, we recognize the importance of understanding how governance and auditing influence investment decisions.

Using data from audited financial statements and public disclosures, we applied linear regression and Baron and Kenny’s mediation analysis. Our results revealed significant fluctuations in investment efficiency, including a sharp drop in 2019 followed by a recovery in 2021. Governance changes, such as longer audit tenures and stricter governance codes, led to adjustments in board structures and audit practices.

While we observed that longer auditor-client relationships improved audit depth, concerns about auditor independence also emerged. Despite the dominance of reputable Big 4 auditors in the market, we found no significant link between corporate governance and investment efficiency, either directly or through audit quality. These findings highlight the need for stronger governance frameworks and more effective audit practices to enhance investment outcomes.
