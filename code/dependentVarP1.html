Investment Efficiency

info Definition of Investment Efficiency

Investment efficiency involves determining the optimal investment level, where any spending aimed at generating returns is considered an investment. Information asymmetry (IA) impacts investment efficiency by influencing decision-making. It allows managers to pursue investments that benefit themselves rather than aligning with the owners' interests, leading to inefficiencies due to differing information levels among stakeholders. (Javed, 2024)

trending_up Measurement of Investment Efficiency

To measure Investment Efficiency, we used Return on Investment (ROI), which is calculated using the formula:
ROI = Net Income ÷ Average Shareholder’s Equity

question_answer Statement of the Problem #1

What is the investment efficiency of listed holding companies on the Philippine Stock Exchange for the years 2017-2023 in terms of return on investment?

Return on Investment
lightbulb_outline Findings of the Study

In 2019, We noticed a sharp decline in ROI, with the minimum ROI dropping to -75.47. This significantly impacted the overall mean ROI, which averaged -5.116 over the seven-year period. This dramatic downturn seemed to point to external shocks, like economic disruptions or poor investment decisions within the industry. The high standard deviation of 17.32 that year highlighted considerable variability in company performance.

After 2019, ROI figures started to stabilize, with smaller fluctuations and reduced variability, as reflected in the lower standard deviations in 2020 (0.04978) and 2022 (0.06263). Despite this stabilization, the negative mean ROI in 2023 (0.0020) suggested that challenges in investment efficiency continued. However, 2021 stood out as a year of significant improvement, with a maximum ROI of 1.97, showing that recovery through adaptive strategies was possible.
