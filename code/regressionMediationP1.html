Effect of Corporate Governance on Audit Quality

info Importance

We recognize that understanding the effect of corporate governance on audit quality is essential because strong governance practices ensure that audits are conducted with integrity, transparency, and independence. This leads to more reliable financial reporting, builds investor trust, and reduces the risk of financial misstatements. Knowing this connection helps us make better decisions by ensuring that companies adhere to high standards of accountability and transparency.

trending_up Measurement

Our study used simple linear regression to test the effect of corporate governance on audit quality.

question_answer Statement of the Problem #4

Does Corporate Governance significantly affect Audit Quality?

help_outline Hypothesis

Corporate Governance has no significant effect on Audit Quality.

lightbulb_outline Findings of the Study

We found that ownership concentration has a significant negative effect on organizational performance (B = -0.910, p < .015), meaning that as ownership becomes more concentrated, performance during audit tenure tends to decline. This could be due to reduced transparency and weaker oversight, as dominant shareholders may prioritize personal interests. However, ownership concentration can strengthen governance when aligned with strong policies, showing its complex nature

We also found that board independence positively impacts organizational performance (B = 1.936, p < .001), highlighting its vital role in improving audit quality and governance. Strong board independence enhances decision-making, reduces managerial discretion, and boosts performance, particularly during audit tenure, where independent members ensure accurate financial reporting.On the other hand, board size (B = -0.063, p < .765) and audit committee membership (B = -0.110, p < .725) did not have a statistically significant effect on performance.

lightbulb_outline Findings of the Study

We found that board size has a statistically significant negative effect on organizational performance (B = -0.288, p < .002), indicating that larger boards may reduce decision-making efficiency, which in turn harms performance. This aligns with Alfraih (2017), who emphasized the role of board size in audit quality, noting that larger boards often struggle with coordination.

In contrast, board independence shows a strong positive effect on performance (B = 0.808, p < .001). Our findings are consistent with Ogoun and Perelayefa (2020), who argued that independent boards enhance accountability, improve audit quality, and support organizational success.

We also found that audit committee membership positively impacts organizational performance (B = 0.603, p < .001), which aligns with Khlif and Samaha (2016), who demonstrated that active audit committees improve internal control and governance quality.

However, ownership concentration does not significantly affect performance (B = 0.270, p < .089), suggesting that ownership concentration may not directly influence audit-related outcomes in this context, supporting Hadiyanto et al. (2018).

lightbulb_outline Findings of the Study

We found that board independence is positively associated with audit fees (B = 1.878, p < .001), suggesting firms with more independent boards tend to incur higher audit costs. This is consistent with Zhang and Yu (2016), who found a similar relationship in the U.S. context.

Audit committee membership also positively affects audit fees (B = 1.074, p < .003), aligning with Afenya et al. (2022), who noted that active audit committees with strong internal controls lead to higher audit fees.

Ownership concentration, however, does not significantly impact audit fees (B = -0.629, p < .142), suggesting its effect on audit costs may be context-dependent, as highlighted by Alfraih (2017).

Finally, board size does not significantly influence audit fees (B = -0.364, p < .138), indicating that the number of board members doesn't substantially affect audit costs, which is in line with prior studies like Alfraih (2017).
