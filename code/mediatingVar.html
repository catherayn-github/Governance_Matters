Audit Quality

info Definition

Audit quality as defined by Wang, (2015) is a fundamental aspect of corporate governance and financial reporting credibility. It encompasses the degree to which an audit effectively evaluates and ensures the accuracy and reliability of financial statements. Audit quality is a multifaceted concept influenced by various factors, including audit tenure, auditor size, and audit fees.

trending_up Measurement

We measure audit quality using the following subvariables:

  • Audit Tenure: Length of auditor-client engagement
  • Auditor Size: Big 4 firms (1 = Big 4, 0 = Non-Big 4)
  • Audit Fee: Annual fees paid to auditors

question_answer Statement of the Problem #3

What is the audit quality of listed holding companies on the Philippine Stock Exchange for the years 2017-2023 in terms of:

  • Audit tenure;
  • Auditor size; and
  • Audit Fees?

Findings of the Study

  • history Audit Tenure

    We observe a rising trend in audit tenure from 2017 to 2023, suggesting infrequent audit firm rotations. This may be due to companies retaining preferred auditors by rotating only the lead audit partner, staying compliant with regulations like the five-year limit for lead partners while maintaining continuity.

    In 2017, the shortest audit tenure was recorded, with a mean of 2.0136, likely due to management changes and the implementation of a new corporate governance code.

    In 2022, audit tenure reached its longest point, with a mean of 2.3697, likely due to the COVID-19 pandemic’s disruptions, which led companies to prioritize stability and continuity in auditor relationships.

  • business Audit Size

    We found that 96 out of 133 audit reports (72.2%) are audited by Big Four accounting firms, while 37 (27.8%) are audited by non-Big Four firms.

    Agency theory suggests that the size of an audit firm impacts audit quality, as a third-party auditor is needed to resolve the conflict of interest between principals and agents.

    Being audited by a Big Four firm signifies credibility and financial integrity, as they are known for their expertise and adherence to international standards.

    Despite higher costs, these firms enhance stakeholder confidence and demonstrate a commitment to governance.

    However, smaller firms, with their flatter structures, offer faster decision-making and more direct client interactions, as noted by Reflex Accounting (2020).

  • attach_money Audit Fee

    We observe a consistent rise in audit fees from 2017 to 2023, primarily driven by inflation, which increases operational costs like wages, office space, and technology, leading businesses to pass on these expenses.

    The 2018 TRAIN Law, food price volatility, and peso depreciation further fueled inflation concerns. In 2023, audit fees peaked with a mean of 14.3377, influenced by both inflation and the lasting impact of the COVID-19 pandemic.

    Additionally, audit fees can increase for larger, more complex firms, requiring more resources and effort. As Shipman (2021) notes, higher fees reflect the added complexity of audits, although fee pressure can sometimes compromise auditor independence and quality.
